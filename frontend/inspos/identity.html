01 — Naming

What to call it.

Three names were developed. Each reflects the same product truth from a different angle. The recommendation is Motif — but all three are defensible depending on the direction you want to take the brand.

★ Recommended
Motif
Fr. — a recurring significant pattern

Your screenshots are not random. Over time, they reveal what you return to — a recurring subject, a collecting instinct, a pattern of attention. Motif names that insight. It's design-adjacent, editorial, phonetically clean, and has no tech-product baggage.

–
Folio
Lat. — a leaf of paper; a collected work

Editorial and archival. Implies a curated body of work rather than a utility tool. Works well if the product leans into the "personal library" metaphor. Risk: slightly passive — a folio is where things go, not where intelligence acts on them.

–
Lume
Lat. — light; to illuminate

Implies that your captures are brought into clarity — things that were dark become visible. Clean, short, distinctive, easy to say. Slight risk: overlaps with existing cosmetic/lighting brands. Best if the product focuses on retrieval and "finding" things.

On domain & handles: motif.app, motif.so, getmotif.com are worth checking. The word is common in design circles which creates brand-fit but also competition for handles. Consider a subdomain strategy like usemotif.com.

02 — Positioning

What it stands for.

Motif is not a productivity app. It is not a note-taking tool. It is not a bookmark manager. It is an intelligence layer for the things you found worth capturing — and the people who find more things worth capturing than they can possibly act on.

"Motif is the editorial layer between what you notice and what you do about it."

Most screenshot apps treat captures as files. Motif treats them as signals. The distinction matters: a file is passive storage. A signal is active data — something to be read, decoded, and acted on. Motif reads the signal so you don't have to.

The product lives in the gap between high-intent capture (you saw something worth saving) and the follow-through that rarely happens (finding it again, acting on it). Motif closes that gap with zero friction.

Brand Pillars
01
Intentional by design

Motif is built for people who capture with purpose. It does not try to organize chaos — it elevates signal. The experience reflects that: deliberate, unhurried, precise.

02
Intelligence without friction

The AI should be invisible. No prompting, no configuration, no learning curve. You capture. It understands. The less the user has to think about the tool, the more they can think about the work.

03
Privacy as a premise, not a feature

On-device processing is not marketed as a checkbox. It is the foundational philosophy: your captures are yours, your patterns belong to you, and no cloud ever sees them.

04
Curation over accumulation

Motif does not want you to save everything. It wants you to find what matters in what you saved. The bias is always toward meaning over volume, toward recall over storage.

03 — Audience

Who it's for.

Motif has a broad use case but a narrow psychographic. The technology works for anyone — the brand speaks to a specific kind of person: someone who notices things, saves them with intention, and is frustrated that intention rarely becomes action.

Primary
The Designer / Creative

Screenshots are a core research tool — references, palettes, UI patterns, editorial layouts. They accumulate hundreds per week with no coherent retrieval system. Motif becomes their research library.

"I know I saved that font pairing somewhere. It was from a magazine redesign I screenshotted in 2022."

Primary
The Developer

Captures error logs, Stack Overflow answers, API docs, UI components. The pain is retrieval: finding that one screenshot with the exact code snippet three months later. Motif parses the code and links out.

"I screenshot that Tailwind trick but I have 4000 screenshots and no idea which folder it's in."

Secondary
The Knowledge Worker

Articles, research threads, prices, flight details, quotes from books. Screenshots are a second brain that never gets reviewed. Motif surfaces them at the right moment, automatically categorized.

"I save everything. I use none of it. I don't even know where to start."

Positioning nuance: The brand should feel like it was built for the designer/creative first — that's the aspirational user that elevates perceived quality. Devs and knowledge workers will follow when they see the precision. Don't lead with developer messaging even if the OCR feature is highly relevant to them.

04 — Visual Identity

How it looks.

The visual language is quiet luxury editorial. This means restraint over decoration, precision over expressiveness, and whitespace as an active element. Think Kinfolk magazine meets a bespoke software interface. Nothing shouts. Everything means something.

Amber / Highlight use

The mark is four quadrant squares — a visual echo of a screenshot grid. Varying opacity creates the sense of some squares being "active" (highlighted, found, surfaced) and others receding. The wordmark uses Cormorant Garamond in a clean roman cut — familiar but not generic.

05 — Voice & Tone

How it speaks.

Motif does not hype. It does not over-explain. It communicates with the confidence of something that knows exactly what it is — and respects that the user also knows what they're looking for. Think editor, not salesperson.

Attribute Write like this Not like this
Precise

Pixel-level OCR. Instant extraction. No training required.

Our powerful AI technology automatically processes all of your screenshots with incredible accuracy!
Understated

Everything you save, finally means something.

The revolutionary AI-powered screenshot manager that will transform your workflow forever.
Dry wit

Your screenshot folder: 4,832 images. Items you've returned to: 9.

We know managing your screenshots can be a challenge. That's why we built Motif to help!
Earned confidence

The archive does the thinking. You do the making.

We believe that everyone deserves a smarter way to organize their digital life.
